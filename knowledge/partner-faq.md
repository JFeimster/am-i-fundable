# Partner FAQ Knowledge File

## Purpose

This FAQ helps referral partners, affiliates, community builders, agencies, and platform operators understand how to use the Funding Readiness Scorecard safely.

## Partner positioning

The scorecard helps partners give their audience a useful readiness experience before routing them into a review workflow.

Good partner use cases:

- Website embed
- Lead magnet
- Community resource
- Newsletter CTA
- Broker referral intake
- Agency white-label tool
- Small business education hub
- E-commerce seller resource
- Contractor or real estate investor prep tool

Unsafe partner use cases:

- Promising approvals
- Publishing private provider names
- Using the score as a final lending decision
- Sending users directly to undisclosed apply links
- Making lender-specific claims in public copy

---

## FAQ

### What does the scorecard do for partners?

It gives your audience a quick funding-readiness assessment and helps route them toward a sensible next step: review, document prep, setup, or nurture.

### Is this a funding offer?

No. It is an educational readiness tool. It does not approve, deny, guarantee, or issue funding.

### Can I embed it on my website?

Yes. The widget can be embedded on partner pages, broker sites, resource pages, landing pages, and education hubs.

### Can I white-label the scorecard?

Yes, with public-safe copy rules. White-label versions should keep all disclaimers and should not imply the partner is making lending decisions.

### What can I say in public promotions?

Safe examples:

- "Check your funding readiness"
- "See potential funding paths"
- "Prepare before you apply"
- "Find out what documents you may need"
- "Get a readiness score in minutes"
- "Request a human review"

Avoid:

- "Get approved"
- "Guaranteed funding"
- "Best rates"
- "Instant approval"
- "Everyone qualifies"
- "No docs needed"
- "Bad credit no problem" when used as a broad promise

### Can I add my own affiliate or apply link?

Not in public scorecard files unless the project owner explicitly approves it and the link is safe for public exposure. The default repo should not include affiliate URLs, apply URLs, provider URLs, or commission information.

### Can I see the internal routing rules?

No. Public partners should only see public-safe path labels, readiness tiers, and general next-step logic. Internal routing, provider fit rules, commissions, and private notes stay server-side or internal-only.

### What data does the scorecard collect?

Public forms may collect basic lead and business-readiness information such as name, email, phone, business name, state, revenue range, time in business, funding purpose, and readiness answers.

Partners should not collect unnecessary sensitive information in public pages.

### What should I tell users about privacy?

Use plain language:

> The scorecard uses your answers to estimate funding readiness and recommend next steps. Do not submit sensitive credentials or full account numbers through public forms.

### What partner audiences work well?

Strong audiences include:

- Small business owners
- Gig workers becoming business owners
- E-commerce sellers
- Contractors
- Trucking and equipment-heavy operators
- Real estate investors
- Startup founders
- Local business communities
- Bookkeepers, coaches, and consultants
- Broker referral partners

### What is the best CTA?

Default CTA:

> Check Your Funding Readiness

Other strong CTAs:

- "Get My Readiness Score"
- "See Potential Funding Paths"
- "Prepare Before You Apply"
- "Request a Funding Readiness Review"

### What should happen after someone submits?

Suggested public-safe partner flow:

1. User completes scorecard.
2. User sees readiness tier and next step.
3. User receives document prep guidance.
4. If appropriate, user requests human review.
5. Internal team reviews the file.
6. Partner receives reporting only if authorized and compliant.

### Can partners use results for segmentation?

Yes, at a high level.

Examples:

- Highly Fundable → strategy review CTA
- Fundable Review → manual review CTA
- Selective Fit → document prep CTA
- Not Ready Yet → education/nurture CTA

Do not expose provider-specific segmentation publicly.

### Can partners publish screenshots or examples?

Yes, but examples must use fake/demo data only and must not imply real funding outcomes.

## Partner-safe promo blurb

```text
Use the Funding Readiness Scorecard to see where your business may stand before applying. Get a readiness score, potential funding path guidance, document prep tips, and a recommended next step. This is not an approval or guarantee of funding.
```

## Partner implementation checklist

- Embed public widget or link to hosted scorecard
- Keep disclaimers visible
- Use safe CTA language
- Do not publish internal routing
- Do not include provider names or affiliate/apply links
- Route review requests to secure intake
- Use demo data for examples
- Track starts, completions, and review requests

---

## Operating guardrails

Use this knowledge for educational funding-readiness guidance only.

Do not present any score, path, checklist, or recommendation as an approval, funding offer, lender decision, underwriting decision, guarantee, or promise of terms. Do not name private providers, expose routing rules, share affiliate/apply links, disclose commissions, or collect unnecessary sensitive information. When a user appears close to a potential funding path, recommend human review before any application or partner handoff.
