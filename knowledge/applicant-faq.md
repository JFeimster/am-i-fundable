# Applicant FAQ Knowledge File

## Purpose

This FAQ helps business owners understand the Funding Readiness Scorecard in clear, public-safe language.

## FAQ

### What is the Funding Readiness Scorecard?

It is a free educational tool that estimates funding readiness based on your answers about revenue, time in business, credit range, bank activity, business setup, funding purpose, requested amount, and common blockers.

It helps you understand what may be worth reviewing next.

### Is this an approval?

No. The scorecard is not an approval, offer, commitment, or guarantee of funding. It is a readiness guide. Any funding path depends on review, documents, business performance, credit profile, program criteria, and other factors.

### Will this hurt my credit?

The public scorecard does not run credit. It uses the answers you provide. If a future review involves a credit check, that should be disclosed separately before it happens.

### What does my score mean?

Your score estimates how ready your file may be for a funding review.

Common tiers:

- Highly Fundable: stronger readiness signals
- Fundable Review: worth manual review
- Selective Fit: possible path, but needs careful review or prep
- Not Ready Yet: better to build the foundation before applying

### What information do I need to complete the scorecard?

You can usually answer with estimates:

- Monthly revenue range
- Time in business
- Estimated credit range
- Business bank account status
- Business structure
- Funding purpose
- Desired amount
- Any major red flags

Do not paste sensitive information like full SSNs, passwords, or bank login details into public chat.

### What happens after I get a score?

You may see:

- Your readiness tier
- Potential funding path families
- Potential blockers
- Recommended next steps
- Suggested documents to prepare
- Option to request human review

### What documents should I prepare?

Common documents include:

- Recent business bank statements
- Business entity details
- EIN, when applicable
- Revenue proof
- Funding amount requested
- Use of funds
- Current business obligations
- Platform sales reports for e-commerce sellers
- Equipment quote or real estate project details, when relevant

### What if I do not have a business bank account?

That may reduce readiness for some paths, but it is fixable. A strong next step is opening a dedicated business bank account and routing business revenue through it.

### What if my credit is not strong?

Credit is one readiness factor, not the whole file. Revenue, bank activity, business structure, documents, and use of funds can also matter. The scorecard can help identify which path or prep step may make sense.

### What if I have recent overdrafts or NSFs?

Recent overdrafts may be a blocker for some funding paths. It helps to document what caused them, whether the issue is resolved, and whether deposits are now stable.

### What if my business is new?

New businesses often need a startup-readiness or setup path before stronger funding paths are realistic. Focus on business structure, banking, early revenue, records, and a realistic funding plan.

### What if I score low?

A low score is not a life sentence. It means the smartest next step may be preparation before applying. You may need to build revenue proof, clean up banking, organize documents, or reduce the funding request.

### Can the scorecard tell me the exact lender or provider?

No. Public scorecard surfaces explain broad funding paths only. Specific partner or provider review should happen privately and only after appropriate documentation and human review.

### Can I use this if I am a gig worker or contractor?

Yes. The scorecard can help gig workers and contractors understand whether they should focus on business setup, banking, revenue documentation, or funding review.

### Can I use this if I sell on Amazon, Shopify, Etsy, eBay, or another marketplace?

Yes. E-commerce and marketplace sellers should be ready to provide recent platform sales reports, bank deposits, inventory needs, and marketplace account status.

### Can I use this for real estate funding?

Yes. Real estate users should be ready with property details, project budget, timeline, exit plan, and relevant business/entity documents.

### How often should I retake the scorecard?

Retake it after meaningful changes, such as:

- 30 to 90 days of stronger deposits
- Opening a business bank account
- Forming an entity
- Improving documentation
- Resolving bank volatility
- Clarifying use of funds
- Reducing or right-sizing the requested amount

## Applicant-safe talk track

```text
The scorecard helps you understand where your file may stand before you apply. It is designed to identify possible paths, blockers, and next steps so you can avoid wasted applications and prepare a stronger review packet.
```

## Operating guardrails

Use this knowledge for educational funding-readiness guidance only.

Do not present any score, path, checklist, or recommendation as an approval, funding offer, lender decision, underwriting decision, guarantee, or promise of terms. Do not name private providers, expose routing rules, share affiliate/apply links, disclose commissions, or collect unnecessary sensitive information. When a user appears close to a potential funding path, recommend human review before any application or partner handoff.
