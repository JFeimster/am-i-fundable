# Broker FAQ Knowledge File

## Purpose

This FAQ helps brokers understand how to position and use the Funding Readiness Scorecard without making unsafe claims or exposing private routing logic.

## Broker positioning

The scorecard is a front-end readiness and routing tool. It helps brokers collect structured borrower context before the first conversation.

It should help answer:

- Is this lead ready for a funding review?
- What documents should be requested first?
- Which broad funding path may fit?
- What blockers need explanation?
- Should this lead go to fast review, manual review, prep, or nurture?

It should not be used as:

- A final eligibility engine
- A lender decisioning tool
- A guarantee machine
- A replacement for human review
- A public directory of private providers

---

## FAQ

### Is the scorecard an approval tool?

No. It is a funding-readiness and pre-review tool. It helps identify possible next steps based on user-provided answers. It does not approve, deny, underwrite, or guarantee funding.

### Can I tell a lead they are qualified if they score high?

No. A high score can justify a faster strategy review, but it is not proof of approval. Use:

> Your answers show stronger readiness signals. The next step is to review documents and confirm the best funding path.

### What should I do with a Highly Fundable result?

Move quickly, but do not overpromise.

Recommended actions:

- Request recent business bank statements
- Confirm requested amount and use of funds
- Review existing obligations
- Verify business details
- Schedule a strategy review
- Keep all claims conditional and documentation-based

### What should I do with a Fundable Review result?

Treat it as a warm lead that needs human context.

Recommended actions:

- Ask what they need funding for
- Request core documents
- Identify the biggest blocker
- Clarify revenue and bank activity
- Decide whether to route, prep, or nurture

### What should I do with a Selective Fit result?

Do not throw the lead into the apply-now wood chipper.

Recommended actions:

- Review the top blockers
- Right-size the funding request
- Offer document prep
- Consider alternate funding path families
- Set expectations carefully
- Create a 30- or 60-day readiness plan if needed

### What should I do with a Not Ready Yet result?

Use nurture and education. Low readiness is not worthless. It is often an undereducated future lead.

Recommended actions:

- Send setup checklist
- Recommend business banking and records cleanup
- Give a document prep plan
- Invite them to re-score later
- Avoid making them feel rejected

### Can I embed this on my broker website?

Yes. Use the embed example and white-label guidance. Keep the public embed language educational and readiness-focused.

Safe embed CTA examples:

- "Check Funding Readiness"
- "Get a Readiness Score"
- "See Potential Funding Paths"
- "Request a Strategy Review"

Avoid:

- "Get Approved Now"
- "Guaranteed Funding"
- "Best Rates"
- "Instant Lender Approval"

### What information should I avoid putting in public pages?

Never expose:

- Provider names
- Provider IDs
- Affiliate links
- Apply links
- Commission data
- Private contacts
- Underwriting notes
- Routing secrets
- Internal partner logic
- Real borrower PII

### Can I customize result language?

Yes, but keep the safety frame intact.

Every result page should include:

> This score is not an approval, offer, commitment, or guarantee of funding.

### Can I use this for paid broker services?

Yes. Strong service packages include:

- Funding readiness review
- Document prep
- Business setup cleanup
- CRM intake
- Follow-up automation
- Broker embed setup
- Manual review workflow

Do not sell it as guaranteed funding access.

### What are the best lead sources for this tool?

Public-safe examples:

- Business owners researching funding
- Gig workers moving toward business banking
- E-commerce sellers needing inventory or ads
- Contractors needing equipment or materials
- Real estate investors preparing project files
- Referral partners with unfiltered leads
- Brokers who need intake discipline

### How should a broker respond when a lead asks, "Do I qualify?"

Use:

> The scorecard suggests a possible readiness path, but qualification depends on review, documentation, program criteria, and the full file. The smart next step is to review your documents and confirm fit.

### How should a broker respond when a lead asks, "What lender will take me?"

Use:

> Before naming any path, we need to review the file safely. The scorecard points to broad funding categories, not final provider placement.

### How should brokers handle sensitive documents?

Use secure intake systems. Do not ask leads to paste full SSNs, bank credentials, account numbers, or private documents into public chat or unsecured forms.

### What is the fastest practical broker workflow?

1. Lead completes scorecard.
2. Broker reviews score tier and blockers.
3. Broker requests targeted documents.
4. Broker confirms use of funds and requested amount.
5. Broker decides: fast review, manual review, prep path, or nurture.
6. Broker follows up with compliant, conditional language.
7. Broker logs outcome for analytics.

## Broker talk track

```text
This scorecard helps us avoid wasting your time. It does not approve or deny funding. It helps us understand your revenue, bank activity, business setup, credit context, and funding purpose so we can recommend the smartest next step.
```

## Broker safety reminder

A broker should never turn a readiness score into a promise. The scorecard is the map, not the money truck.

---

## Operating guardrails

Use this knowledge for educational funding-readiness guidance only.

Do not present any score, path, checklist, or recommendation as an approval, funding offer, lender decision, underwriting decision, guarantee, or promise of terms. Do not name private providers, expose routing rules, share affiliate/apply links, disclose commissions, or collect unnecessary sensitive information. When a user appears close to a potential funding path, recommend human review before any application or partner handoff.
