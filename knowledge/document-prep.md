# Document Prep Knowledge File

## Purpose

This file helps GPT assistants and public pages explain which documents a business owner may need before a funding-readiness review.

Document prep is not a funding application. It is a way to reduce friction, clarify the file, and avoid the classic "I swear the money is real, it is just shy" routine.

## Universal document checklist

Most users should start with the basic readiness packet:

- Government-issued ID for business owner or authorized signer
- Business legal name and DBA, if any
- EIN or tax ID, when applicable
- Business bank account details
- Last 3 to 6 months of business bank statements
- Basic revenue summary
- Funding amount requested
- Intended use of funds
- Business address and contact details
- Ownership percentage and signer authority
- Existing business debt or cash advances, if any
- Explanation for recent bank volatility or unusual deposits, if relevant

Do not ask for full SSNs, passwords, online banking credentials, or unnecessary sensitive documents in public GPT chat. Sensitive items should only be handled through secure intake channels.

## By funding purpose

### Working capital

Useful documents:

- 3 to 6 months of business bank statements
- Revenue summary by month
- Current obligations or business debt summary
- Payroll, vendor, inventory, or operating expense explanation
- Basic profit-and-loss summary if available
- Recent invoices or purchase orders if relevant

Why these matter:

Working capital review often depends on business cash flow, deposit consistency, existing obligations, and the urgency of the use case.

Safe copy:

> For a working capital review, start with recent bank statements, a clear use of funds, and a realistic amount tied to monthly revenue.

### Inventory or materials

Useful documents:

- Supplier quote or invoice
- Purchase order or sales forecast
- Inventory plan
- Recent sales or revenue proof
- Bank statements
- Explanation of expected turnover or margin

Why these matter:

Inventory funding needs a credible path from purchase to sales, not just "buy stuff and pray to the algorithm gods."

Safe copy:

> If funding is for inventory or materials, document the supplier cost, expected sales cycle, and recent revenue that supports the request.

### Equipment, vehicle, or truck

Useful documents:

- Equipment quote, invoice, or seller details
- Description of business use
- Revenue or contracts tied to the asset
- Business bank statements
- Insurance or registration details when relevant
- Down payment availability if applicable

Why these matter:

Equipment review usually needs the asset details and the business reason the asset can support revenue or operations.

Safe copy:

> For equipment funding, prepare the quote, business use case, and proof that the asset supports business operations.

### E-commerce or marketplace growth

Useful documents:

- Platform sales reports
- Store analytics or revenue exports
- Bank statements showing deposits
- Inventory purchase plan
- Advertising plan if funding is for growth
- Marketplace account status details
- Refund, chargeback, or suspension explanation if relevant

Why these matter:

Marketplace health and sales consistency can be as important as bank deposits.

Safe copy:

> For e-commerce funding review, recent platform sales reports and bank statements help connect store activity to funding readiness.

### Real estate purchase, bridge, or project

Useful documents:

- Property address or project summary
- Purchase contract, if available
- Rehab or project budget
- Photos or property details
- Exit strategy summary
- Rent roll, lease, or ARV estimate when relevant
- Entity details and bank statements
- Proof of funds or reserves when applicable

Why these matter:

Real estate review depends on the asset, project economics, use of funds, and exit plan.

Safe copy:

> For a real estate funding review, gather the property details, budget, timeline, and exit plan before requesting a path review.

### Startup launch capital

Useful documents:

- Business plan or one-page launch plan
- Startup budget
- Entity and EIN documents, if formed
- Business bank account records, if opened
- Founder resume or experience summary
- Early sales, contracts, waitlist, invoices, or traction proof
- Personal credit context if voluntarily relevant

Why these matter:

Pre-revenue businesses often need stronger founder, credit, collateral, or traction context because operating history is limited.

Safe copy:

> For startup funding readiness, focus on setup, traction proof, founder context, and a realistic launch budget.

### Business credit setup

Useful documents:

- Entity formation records
- EIN confirmation
- Business bank account confirmation
- Business address and phone setup
- Website or basic online presence
- Vendor account records, if any
- Payment history documentation

Why these matter:

Business credit readiness starts with clean identity, banking, and records. It should not be presented as a shortcut around revenue or repayment ability.

Safe copy:

> For business credit setup, organize entity, EIN, business banking, address, and payment-history records before seeking larger credit paths.

### Debt consolidation or refinancing

Useful documents:

- Current debt schedule
- Current balances
- Payment amounts and frequency
- Payoff letters if available
- Business bank statements
- Revenue summary
- Reason for refinance or consolidation

Why these matter:

Refinance review requires understanding whether new capital improves cash flow or simply adds more pressure.

Safe copy:

> For consolidation or refinance review, document existing obligations and monthly payment pressure before exploring a next step.

## Document prep by score tier

### Highly Fundable

Focus:

- Fast review packet
- Bank statements
- Funding purpose
- Amount requested
- Existing obligations
- Entity and contact info

Recommended message:

> Your score suggests you may be ready for a faster strategy review if the documents support your answers. Start with bank statements, use of funds, and current obligations.

### Fundable Review

Focus:

- Clarifying documents
- Explanation of any caution flags
- Revenue proof
- Bank statement review
- Right-sized request

Recommended message:

> Your file may be worth review, but the documents need to explain the full picture. Gather bank statements, revenue proof, and context for any blockers.

### Selective Fit

Focus:

- Blocker cleanup
- Smaller or staged request
- Business setup gaps
- Bank volatility explanations
- Nurture plan

Recommended message:

> Before applying, build a cleaner review packet and address the most obvious blockers. This can prevent wasted applications.

### Not Ready Yet

Focus:

- Foundational setup
- Business bank account
- Revenue tracking
- Basic records
- Re-score plan

Recommended message:

> Applying now may not be the smartest move. Build the foundation first, then re-score after stronger activity and documentation.

## Sensitive information handling

Do not ask users to paste:

- Full Social Security numbers
- Bank login credentials
- Tax return pages with unnecessary sensitive details
- Full account numbers
- Passwords
- Driver's license images in public chat
- Private borrower documents directly into public website surfaces

Safer public-chat phrasing:

> Do not paste sensitive personal or banking information here. Use a secure intake channel if a human review requires documents.

## Document request template

```text
To prepare for a funding-readiness review, gather:

1. Last 3 to 6 months of business bank statements
2. Monthly revenue summary
3. Funding amount requested
4. Clear use of funds
5. Business entity and EIN details, if applicable
6. Current business debt or payment obligations
7. Any explanation for recent bank volatility, NSFs, liens, or unusual activity

This checklist is for readiness preparation only. It is not an approval, funding offer, or guarantee.
```

## Operating guardrails

Use this knowledge for educational funding-readiness guidance only.

Do not present any score, path, checklist, or recommendation as an approval, funding offer, lender decision, underwriting decision, guarantee, or promise of terms. Do not name private providers, expose routing rules, share affiliate/apply links, disclose commissions, or collect unnecessary sensitive information. When a user appears close to a potential funding path, recommend human review before any application or partner handoff.
