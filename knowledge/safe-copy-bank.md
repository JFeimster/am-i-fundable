# Safe Copy Bank

## Purpose

This file provides reusable public-safe phrases for the Funding Readiness Scorecard ecosystem.

Use these phrases in public pages, GPT replies, result explanations, email snippets, partner pages, broker pages, and knowledge-base responses.

## Core safety line

Use this often:

> This score is not an approval, offer, commitment, or guarantee of funding.

Alternative versions:

- "This is a readiness estimate based on your answers, not a lending decision."
- "Any next step is subject to review, documentation, and program criteria."
- "A human review should confirm fit before you apply."
- "Funding paths may vary based on the full file and supporting documents."

## General scorecard positioning

- "Check your funding readiness before you apply."
- "Get a clear readiness score based on your answers."

## Tier copy

### Highly Fundable

- "Your answers show stronger funding-readiness signals."
- "You may be ready for a faster strategy review if documentation supports your answers."
- "The next step is to confirm documents, use of funds, and fit."
- "Strong score. Still not a blank check from the money gods."

### Fundable Review

- "Your file may be worth a manual funding-readiness review."
- "There are useful signals, but the details need context."
- "A human review can help confirm the best next step."
- "This is the 'talk to a human before making a mess' zone."

### Selective Fit

- "Your file may be a selective fit and should be reviewed carefully."
- "The next step is to identify blockers and right-size the request."
- "A prep-first path may improve your odds of a cleaner review later."
- "Do not apply everywhere like a raccoon found a keyboard."

### Not Ready Yet

- "Applying right now may create wasted effort."
- "The smarter next step is to build your readiness foundation."
- "Focus on banking, records, revenue proof, and document prep."
- "This is not rejection. It is the warm-up lap."

## Funding path copy

### Working capital

- "Working capital may be worth reviewing if you have active revenue and a clear operating need."
- "Recent bank statements and current obligations help confirm fit."
- "This path is commonly used for payroll, inventory, materials, or short-cycle operating gaps."

### Line of credit or revolving capital

- "A revolving capital path may fit businesses with consistent revenue and recurring cash-flow needs."
- "Clean bank activity, stronger credit, and organized records can matter here."

### Startup readiness

- "For new businesses, the best next step may be setup and traction before applying."
- "A launch budget, entity setup, business banking, and early sales proof can improve readiness."

### Equipment or asset funding

- "Equipment funding may be worth reviewing when the asset has a clear business use."
- "Prepare the quote, invoice, or equipment details before review."

### E-commerce capital

- "E-commerce funding review often depends on store sales, platform health, bank deposits, and inventory needs."
- "Recent platform reports and bank statements help connect sales to readiness."

### Real estate capital

- "A real estate funding path may be worth reviewing when the property, budget, timeline, and exit plan are clear."
- "Project details matter more than vague ambition wearing a hard hat."

### Business credit setup

- "Business credit readiness starts with clean entity, EIN, business banking, and records."
- "This path is preparation, not a promise of credit or funding."

## Document prep copy

- "Gather recent business bank statements before review."
- "Prepare a clear use-of-funds explanation."
- "Document existing business obligations."
- "For equipment, gather quotes or invoices."
- "For e-commerce, gather platform sales reports."
- "For real estate, gather property details and project budget."
- "Do not paste sensitive account numbers or credentials into public chat."

## Red flag copy

- "This may be a potential blocker, but context matters."
- "The file may need explanation before review."
- "A prep-first step can prevent wasted applications."
- "Recent bank volatility should be documented."
- "Existing payment obligations should be reviewed before taking on more debt."
- "A lower score is a signal to prepare, not a reason to disappear into the swamp."

## Operating guardrails

Use this knowledge for educational funding-readiness guidance only.

Do not present any score, path, checklist, or recommendation as an approval, funding offer, lender decision, underwriting decision, guarantee, or promise of terms. Do not name private providers, expose routing rules, share affiliate/apply links, disclose commissions, or collect unnecessary sensitive information. When a user appears close to a potential funding path, recommend human review before any application or partner handoff.
