# Nurture Paths Knowledge File

## Purpose

This file defines public-safe nurture paths for users who are not ready for immediate funding review or who need preparation before applying.

Nurture is not failure. Nurture is where raw leads become educated future buyers instead of getting thrown into the CRM swamp to become fossil fuel.

## Nurture principles

- Do not shame the user.
- Give one practical next step at a time.
- Make the path measurable.
- Invite the user to re-score after progress.
- Keep all language educational.
- Do not promise future approval or funding.
- Do not expose internal routing logic.

## Main nurture paths

### 1. Business setup foundation

Best for users with:

- No formal business yet
- No EIN
- No business bank account
- Mixed personal and business finances
- Unclear business identity

Primary goal:

Build a basic business profile that can support cleaner financial records.

Suggested steps:

1. Confirm business name and structure.
2. Set up or verify EIN, if applicable.
3. Open a dedicated business bank account.
4. Create a basic business address, phone, and email setup.
5. Separate business revenue and expenses.
6. Track deposits for 30 to 90 days.
7. Re-score after consistent activity.

Safe message:

> Your best next step may be business setup and banking before applying. A cleaner foundation can make future review easier, but it does not guarantee funding.

### 2. Revenue proof path

Best for users with:

- Low or inconsistent revenue
- No recent deposits
- Platform income not documented
- Cash sales with weak records
- New or seasonal business

Primary goal:

Create clearer proof of business activity.

Suggested steps:

1. Track revenue weekly.
2. Route business income through a business account.
3. Save invoices, receipts, contracts, and platform reports.
4. Create a monthly revenue summary.
5. Document seasonality or gaps.
6. Re-score after 30 to 90 days of cleaner records.

Safe message:

> Revenue proof can improve funding-readiness review. Start by documenting deposits, invoices, platform sales, and monthly revenue trends.

### 3. Bank activity cleanup path

Best for users with:

- Recent NSFs
- Overdrafts
- Unstable balances
- New bank account
- High payment pressure
- Mixed personal and business activity

Primary goal:

Improve bank-statement presentation and explain volatility.

Suggested steps:

1. Identify recent bank volatility.
2. Stop unnecessary account mixing.
3. Document why NSFs or overdrafts happened.
4. Build a 30-day clean activity streak.
5. Review existing daily or weekly obligations.
6. Avoid taking on more payment pressure without review.
7. Re-score after bank activity improves.

Safe message:

> Recent bank volatility may affect readiness. Focus on cleaner deposits, fewer overdrafts, and a clear explanation before seeking review.

### 4. Document prep path

Best for users with:

- Good signals but missing paperwork
- Unclear funding purpose
- No bank statements ready
- No equipment quote, platform report, or property details
- Incomplete business records

Primary goal:

Build a review-ready document packet.

Suggested steps:

1. Gather recent bank statements.
2. Write a clear use-of-funds note.
3. List current business obligations.
4. Prepare revenue proof.
5. Add purpose-specific documents.
6. Request manual review only after the packet is coherent.

Safe message:

> Your file may need documents more than drama. Gather the core packet first so a human can review the real picture.

### 5. Right-size the request path

Best for users with:

- Funding request much larger than revenue supports
- New business asking for large capital
- No clear use of funds
- High existing obligations
- Vague growth plan

Primary goal:

Align the requested amount with documented need and repayment capacity.

Suggested steps:

1. Define the exact use of funds.
2. Separate "need" from "want."
3. Estimate return or operating impact.
4. Compare amount to monthly revenue.
5. Consider phased funding or smaller initial request.
6. Re-score with a realistic amount.

Safe message:

> The funding amount should match the business file. A smaller or phased request may create a cleaner review path.

### 6. Red flag context path

Best for users with:

- Tax lien
- Open bankruptcy
- Recent missed payments
- Existing daily payment obligations
- Marketplace suspension
- Heavy debt pressure

Primary goal:

Document context and avoid reckless applications.

Suggested steps:

1. Identify the red flag.
2. Document current status.
3. Clarify whether it is active, resolved, disputed, or under a plan.
4. Gather supporting documents.
5. Avoid broad applications until reviewed.
6. Consider professional guidance for legal, tax, or credit issues.

Safe message:

> A red flag does not always end the conversation, but it needs context. Document the issue before applying so the file can be reviewed honestly.

### 7. E-commerce seller prep path

Best for users with:

- Platform revenue
- Inventory need
- Advertising or growth need
- Marketplace volatility
- Refund or chargeback concerns
- Suspended account

Primary goal:

Connect marketplace activity to funding readiness.

Suggested steps:

1. Gather platform sales reports.
2. Confirm marketplace account status.
3. Track deposits into business banking.
4. Document inventory need and margin.
5. Review refunds, chargebacks, and suspensions.
6. Re-score after sales and deposits are clean.

Safe message:

> E-commerce review works better when store sales, bank deposits, and inventory needs tell the same story.

### 8. Contractor and equipment prep path

Best for users with:

- Equipment need
- Trucking or contractor business
- Project-based revenue
- Materials need
- Vehicle or machinery purchase

Primary goal:

Document the asset and its business use.

Suggested steps:

1. Get equipment quote or invoice.
2. Explain business use.
3. Document current revenue or contracts.
4. Prepare bank statements.
5. Estimate how the asset supports operations.
6. Re-score with the specific equipment amount.

Safe message:

> Equipment-related funding review needs more than "I need the thing." Prepare the quote, use case, and revenue connection.

### 9. Real estate project prep path

Best for users with:

- Property funding need
- Fix-and-flip plan
- Rental or bridge scenario
- Commercial property idea
- Project without clear budget

Primary goal:

Turn a real estate idea into a reviewable project file.

Suggested steps:

1. Identify property address.
2. Create project budget.
3. Estimate timeline.
4. Document purchase, rehab, or refinance plan.
5. Clarify exit strategy.
6. Gather entity and bank records.
7. Re-score with project details.

Safe message:

> Real estate funding review needs a property, budget, timeline, and exit plan. Vibes do not appraise well.

## Nurture sequence framework

### 7-day follow-up

Purpose:

- Deliver score summary
- Identify one blocker
- Give a short checklist
- Invite the user to reply with the main funding purpose

### 14-day follow-up

Purpose:

- Ask what progress has been made
- Send purpose-specific document prep
- Encourage bank/revenue organization
- Offer re-score after updates

### 30-day follow-up

Purpose:

- Revisit readiness
- Ask about revenue changes
- Ask about banking and documents
- Invite manual review if signals improved

### 60-day follow-up

Purpose:

- Encourage re-score
- Offer checklist review
- Continue education
- Move inactive users to light nurture

### 90-day follow-up

Purpose:

- Re-score prompt
- Ask if the use of funds changed
- Identify whether the file is now review-ready
- Keep the door open without pressure

## Nurture message templates

### Low score response

```text
Based on your answers, applying right now may create wasted effort. The better next step is a prep-first plan focused on business setup, banking, revenue proof, and documentation. This is not a rejection or funding decision.
```

### Bank cleanup response

```text
Recent bank volatility may affect funding readiness. Focus on cleaner account activity, consistent deposits, and a written explanation for any recent overdrafts before requesting review.
```

### Re-score invitation

```text
After 30 to 90 days of stronger records, retake the scorecard. A new score can help identify whether your next step should shift from preparation to review.
```

### Manual review bridge

```text
If you have documents that explain the full picture, a human review may help determine whether there is a realistic next step. The scorecard alone is not an approval or guarantee.
```

## Operating guardrails

Use this knowledge for educational funding-readiness guidance only.

Do not present any score, path, checklist, or recommendation as an approval, funding offer, lender decision, underwriting decision, guarantee, or promise of terms. Do not name private providers, expose routing rules, share affiliate/apply links, disclose commissions, or collect unnecessary sensitive information. When a user appears close to a potential funding path, recommend human review before any application or partner handoff.
