Funding readiness, not funding promises

Know what lenders and funding partners usually want to see before you ask for capital.

This knowledge base explains common readiness signals: revenue, time in business, banking activity, credit profile, business structure, funding purpose, and documentation.

Check Funding Readiness

Core readiness signals

Revenue

Consistent monthly revenue can help show operating activity and repayment capacity for certain funding paths.

Time in business

Longer operating history may open more review paths. Newer businesses may need more setup and documentation.

Bank activity

Business bank statements often help reviewers understand deposits, cash flow, NSFs, and account stability.

Credit profile

Credit can affect which paths may be worth reviewing, but readiness is broader than a single score.

Business structure

Entity setup, EIN usage, business banking, and clean records can reduce friction during review.

Funding purpose

A clear use of funds helps route the conversation toward working capital, equipment, inventory, growth, or prep.

What this page does not do

This page does not approve applications, guarantee funding, provide underwriting decisions, quote rates, or replace professional advice. It helps business owners organize the basics before a review.
