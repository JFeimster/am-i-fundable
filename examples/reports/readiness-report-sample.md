## Potential funding paths

### 1. Working Capital

This may be worth reviewing when a business has active revenue, operating history, and a short-cycle cash-flow need such as payroll, materials, inventory, or near-term growth expenses.

**Why it may fit this demo profile:**

- The business has more than 24 months of operating history.
- Revenue appears active enough to review against the requested range.
- The funding purpose is tied to business operations, not vague personal use.

### 2. Inventory or Growth Capital

This may be worth reviewing when funding is tied to inventory, purchase orders, marketplace demand, or growth activity that can be documented.

**Why it may fit this demo profile:**

- The requested use of funds is specific.
- Inventory needs can often be supported with invoices, supplier quotes, or sales trends.
- A review can compare the requested range against bank activity and recent sales.

## Potential blockers to review

Even a strong-looking file can hit speed bumps. The goal is to find them before the application circus starts juggling knives.

- Requested amount should be compared against recent monthly revenue.
- Bank statements should be reviewed for NSFs, negative days, inconsistent deposits, or unusual withdrawals.
- Existing obligations should be reviewed to understand cash-flow pressure.
- Documentation quality may determine whether the next step is fast review or prep-first.

---

## Recommended documents

Prepare these before requesting review:

1. Most recent 3–6 months of business bank statements.
2. Government-issued ID for owner verification.
3. Business entity and EIN details.
4. Basic profit-and-loss statement or revenue summary.
5. Inventory quote, supplier invoice, purchase order, or growth plan.
6. Current debt or advance summary, if any.
7. Payment processor or marketplace statements, if relevant.

## Recommended next step

**Request a funding readiness review.**

A human review should confirm:

- Revenue trend.

- Requested amount realism.
- Funding purpose.
- Existing obligations.
- Document quality.
- Any hidden risk factors that were not captured by the scorecard.
