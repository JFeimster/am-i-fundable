Frequently asked questions

Funding readiness, minus the smoke machine.

Is the Funding Readiness Scorecard an approval?

No. It is an educational readiness tool. It is not an approval, offer, commitment to lend, or guarantee of funding.

Does the scorecard run my credit?

No. The frontend scorecard uses the answers you provide. A later review process may request more information, but this static page does not run credit.

What does a high score mean?

A high score means your answers show stronger readiness signals. It does not mean funding is guaranteed or that a specific option is available.

What if I get a low score?

A low score usually means the file needs preparation: cleaner bank activity, revenue proof, business setup, better documentation, or a more realistic funding amount.

Which funding paths are covered?

The scorecard explains broad categories such as working capital, structured growth capital, startup or credit leverage, equipment, real estate, and e-commerce seller capital.

Can brokers use this tool?

Yes. Brokers can use it as a safer intake layer to understand lead readiness before a conversation. Broker workflows should still rely on human review.

Can partners embed the scorecard?

Yes. The widget can be embedded on public pages using a browser-safe surface. It should not expose internal registries or private partner data.

What documents should I prepare?

Common documents include recent bank statements, revenue proof, entity details, funding purpose notes, and any project-specific documents such as equipment quotes or property details.

Is this legal, tax, credit repair, or financial advice?

No. The scorecard provides educational funding-readiness guidance only.

What happens after I submit my information?

Your information may be used to provide a score summary, recommend a next step, or support a human review. See the privacy page for data-use details.
